Bacharuddin Jusuf Habibie

The author of the Habibie Factor, Theorem, and Method for the safety aspects in the aircraft industry.

Here are timelines of B.J. Habibie:

  • 1936Born on June 25th, Parepare, Dutch East Indies (now South Sulawesi)
  • 1954Graduated from Bandung Institute of Technology (ITB), then pursuing study of aviation technology at Delft University of Technology, Netherlands
  • 1960He received an engineer's degree (majoring in aircraft construction) with the title Diplom-Ingenieur in RWTH Aachen University, West Germany. (Rheinisch-Westfälische Technische Hochschule)
  • 1965Still in the same university, he received Doktor Ingenieur (Dr.-Ing.) title in aerospace engineering with the grade of "very good"
  • 1974Promoted to vice president in Messerschmitt-Bölkow-Blohm in Hamburg, while on the development of Airbus A-300B aircraft, he developed the crack propagation theory, thus earned the nickname "Mr. Crack"
  • 1974Returned to Indonesia, and become the founder of the Assessment and Application of Technology (BPPT)
  • 1976Promoted to CEO of Nurtanio Aircraft Industry (IPTN)
  • 1978Promoted to 4th minister of research and technology of Indonesia (Kemenristek)
  • 1980Designed and led the project of the Indonesia's first aircraft IPTN N-250 Gatotkaca, the fastest in its class at that time
  • 1991Oversaw 10 state-owned industries (BUMN) including ship and train-building, steel, arms, communications, and energy industry
  • 1991Created scholarships programs for high school graduates (OFP, STMDP, and STAID), and providing them to earn their bachelor's degrees in the STEM fields and other technical professionals to continue their study for master's and doctorate program overseas
  • 1992Has filed 46 global patents and received the von Karman Award from the International Council Aeronautical Science (ICAS)
  • 1998Elected as vice president of Indonesia in March, and 2 months later on May 21st, he became the 3rd president of Indonesia, he is the first president with technocrat backgrounds
  • 1999Relinquished his position on October 20th, his presidency lasted for about 512 days, making it the shortest in Indonesian history, then he established an independent think tank called The Habibie Center, which respect and uphold human rights, modernizing culture, democracy, improving human resources in science, technology, media, information, marine resources fields based on morality, religious values, and cultural integrity
  • 2019Passed away on September 11th at age of 83 because of heart failure
